FAQ

What are real estate limited partnerships?
A real estate limited partnership (LP) is an investment vehicle that optimizes returns through the selling and/or leasing of properties. Investors are able to enjoy the benefits of a direct investment in tangible assets without many of the complexities that normally accompany such investments. Limited partnerships not only offer higher returns than traditional real estate investment trusts (REITs) but greater tax benefits as well. As a real estate limited partnership shareholder, you own a portion of a company that is solely structured for the purpose of developing a particular property.

The ventures sponsored by Wheat Capital Management, LLC. (WCM) are generally formed as limited partnerships. The partnerships’ sponsors pool investment funds to develop projects and/or to re-develop existing properties. The general partner, who, in the case of a limited partnership is known as a sponsor, is responsible for operating and sustaining the company’s assets. Limited partners, which are the investors of the LPs, are not liable for losses beyond their original investment and do not participate in the partnership management.

What is a Private Placement?
A private placement is a type of investment offering that is exempt from registration with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). Public companies must register with the SEC, which can be expensive and time consuming. Typically, real estate projects are structured as LPs and capitalized through a Private Placement Memorandum (PPM). Such PPMs still undergo standard due diligence but present an opportunity to save time and money for all parties involved.
